About Fishers Island

We are excited to have our wedding on Fishers Island, the place we are happy to call our home. Fishers Island is about eight miles long and, though part of New York, is only accessible from Connecticut.

The primary method to get to the island is via the Fishers Island Ferry, which travels between New London, CT and Silver Eel Pond on the west end of the island. The ferry transports vehicles and walk-on passengers. We will also charter the Popeye to cary walk-ons from Noank to the "Mobil Dock" the morning of the wedding and back again the same evening.

The weather on a small island can be unpredictable. In the winter, it is equally likely for school to be canceled from high winds and rough seas than from snow. Rain can break out at any time, even in clear skies. In the summer, the humidity can make it rain indoors during the day and the cool sea breeze quickly saps any warmth from the sun after it sets.

The island is a small, tight-knit community where everybody knows your name (or why you’re visiting). We’re extremely grateful to our host families, St. John’s Church, the Hay Harbor Club and the Community Center Café for accommodating us.
